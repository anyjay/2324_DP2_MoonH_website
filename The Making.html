The Making

Read about how the contents of this music box came to be:

The film adaptation, directed by Robert Wise and produced by 20th Century Fox, was greenlit in the early 1960s. Julie Andrews, fresh from her success in "Mary Poppins," was cast as Maria, while Christopher Plummer was chosen to play Captain Georg von Trapp. Filming took place in various locations, including Salzburg, Austria, and the 20th Century Fox studios in California.

During production, the crew faced several challenges, including weather-related delays and logistical issues in Austria. However, the picturesque settings, combined with the memorable score by Rodgers and Hammerstein, contributed to the film's charm. The film’s musical numbers, such as "Do-Re-Mi," "My Favorite Things," and "Climb Ev'ry Mountain," became iconic.

Lehman once said of the music:

“[The Sound of Music] is not to be a picture in which we feel slightly apologetic every time anyone sings. On the contrary, our aim is to be slightly apologetic every time anyone talks.”

After...

Released in 1965, "The Sound of Music" received critical acclaim and was a box office sensation. It won five Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director. The film's success solidified its status as a classic, and it remains one of the most beloved musicals of all time, celebrated for its heartwarming story, captivating performances, and timeless music.
