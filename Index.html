A Note from The Keeper

One summer night, my family sat down to watch our next family movie night film. This one was a staple movie, our dad had told us. The Sound of Music. I had never heard of it, but I was enticed by my own unfamiliarity with something supposedly so culturally significant.

Five years later, I cannot imagine myself without this movie. No movie has ever surpassed the love I feel for the Sound of Music. It has been embedded in my personality all these years, from its nostalgia to the authenticity of its music, and especially its heartwarming characters that have remained in my heart ever since I first watched the film.

In retrospect, I suspect that on that quiet summer night, I had, unbeknownst to me, felt the deep, imminent sensation that I was about to witness a consequential turning point in my life. This movie was my first grand introduction to a world of film, setting the stage for a world I would not revisit until my post-pandemic adolescent years—during which I’m still navigating my passions for the art of video and filmmaking. In this critical stage, I felt obliged to call tribute to the very inspiration that started it all.

Here is a digital time-capsule dedicated to a timeless movie that hopefully continues to leave its legacy as a timeless treasure in cinematic history. As the keeper of this music box, I will do my best to commemorate the Sound of Music’s memory.

- Hyaemhin Moon

Avid Sound of Music enthusiast since 2019
